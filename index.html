How it works
01
Write as you normally would

Type your message directly into Aperta. No special instructions. No format to follow. Your voice, your words — exactly as you'd send them. Aperta reads along, silently.

02
See it through their eyes

Aperta shows you the phrases your reader might interpret differently — context you've assumed, gaps they'll fill in on their own, and questions that could generate a reply you weren't expecting. Not what you meant wrong. What they might read differently.

03
Close the gap, keep your voice

Answer a few targeted questions. Aperta produces one refined version — still you, still your message — with the ambiguities resolved before they become a thread of twelve follow-ups. Send it or keep the original. Always your call.

The name

From the universal
language — open.

Aperta is Esperanto for open. Esperanto was built to remove barriers between people — not to change who they are, but to let them be understood. An aperture lets light through unchanged. That is what Aperta does to your message.

The people who communicate clearly are not the most vulnerable but the most efficient. We choose to be fully visible in what we say — not as an act of courage, but as an act of respect. Because we need to cooperate, not compete. That is how things actually move forward.
— The philosophy behind Aperta, Cyncerity founders
